# How Solar Companies Can Get More Leads from Google Search

Most solar companies rely on three lead sources: door-to-door sales, paid ads, and lead aggregators like EnergySage. All three have the same problem — they stop working the moment you stop paying.

SEO is different. It builds an organic lead pipeline that compounds over time. One of our solar clients went from 3 organic leads per month to 47 in under six months. Here's exactly how they did it — and how you can replicate the strategy.

## Why Google is the Best Lead Source for Solar

When a homeowner searches "solar panel installation near me," they're not casually browsing. They're actively considering a $20,000+ purchase. These are the most qualified leads you can get.

Compare that to:
- **Door-to-door:** Cold outreach to uninterested homeowners. Closing rate: 2-5%.
- **Paid leads (EnergySage, etc.):** Shared with 3-5 other installers. You're competing on price before you even talk.
- **Google Ads:** $30-$80 per click with no guarantee of conversion. Stop paying, stop getting leads.
- **Organic Google search:** Exclusive leads who chose YOUR company. Closing rate: 15-25%. And the cost per lead drops every month.

## The 6-Step Strategy That Generates 40+ Solar Leads Per Month

### Step 1: Optimize Your Google Business Profile

Your GBP is the single most important asset for local solar leads. When someone searches "solar installers near me," the Map Pack (the top 3 Google Maps results) captures 42% of all clicks.

**Optimization checklist:**
- Complete every field: business name, categories, service areas, hours, attributes
- Add 20+ high-quality photos of installations, your team, and before/after projects
- Post weekly updates (project completions, seasonal promotions, industry news)
- Respond to every review within 24 hours
- Add your services with descriptions and pricing ranges

### Step 2: Build Service Area Pages

If you serve 15 cities, you need 15 city-specific service pages. Each page should:

- Target "[city] solar panel installation" as the primary keyword
- Include city-specific content (local utility rates, state incentives, weather/sun data)
- Feature testimonials from customers in that area
- Include a clear call-to-action with phone number and form
- Use local schema markup

**Example:** Instead of one generic "Solar Installation" page, create:
- "Solar Panel Installation in Phoenix, AZ"
- "Solar Panel Installation in Scottsdale, AZ"
- "Solar Panel Installation in Tempe, AZ"

Each page ranks independently for its target city keyword.

### Step 3: Create High-Intent Content

Blog content captures leads at every stage of the buying journey. Focus on the questions homeowners actually ask:

**Top-of-funnel (awareness):**
- "Are solar panels worth it in [state]?"
- "How do solar panels work?"

**Middle-of-funnel (consideration):**
- "Solar panel cost in [state] — 2026 pricing guide"
- "Best solar companies in [city]"
- "Solar lease vs. buy: which is better?"

**Bottom-of-funnel (decision):**
- "What to expect during solar installation"
- "Solar panel financing options in [state]"
- "How to choose a solar installer"

### Step 4: Earn Reviews Systematically

Reviews are the second most important ranking factor for the Map Pack. More reviews = higher rankings = more leads.

**Review strategy:**
- Send a review request via text/email within 48 hours of installation completion
- Make it easy — include a direct link to your Google review page
- Follow up once if no response after 5 days
- Never offer incentives for reviews (violates Google's policies)
- Respond to every review — positive and negative

Target: 5-10 new reviews per month minimum.

### Step 5: Build Local Authority with Backlinks

Backlinks from local and industry websites signal to Google that your company is credible.

**Quick wins:**
- Solar industry directories (SEIA member directory, SolarReviews)
- Local Chamber of Commerce and BBB
- Sponsor local events and get listed on their websites
- Partner with roofers and electricians for referral link exchanges
- Get featured in local news for community solar projects

### Step 6: Track Everything and Optimize Monthly

What gets measured gets improved. Track:

- **Map Pack rankings** for "[city] solar installation" keywords
- **Organic leads** (calls, forms, chats attributed to organic search)
- **Cost per lead** vs. paid channels
- **Review count and rating** growth
- **Top-performing content** and double down on what works
